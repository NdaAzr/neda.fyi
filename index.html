Dr Neda Azarmehr

I am an Assistant Professor in the Data Science and AI at The University of Sheffield, Information School. My current research focuses on developing computational models using advanced computer vision and multimodal artificial intelligence (AI) to support clinicians in decision-making. My expertise is research and development experience in medical imaging, processing, deep learning, computer vision, and computer-aided diagnosis techniques. Examples of my research include automated echocardiography view detection with a focus on lightweight deep neural networks, left ventricle segmentation, speckle tracking using echocardiographic images, automated detection of colonic polyps, and the segmentation and quantification of digital pathology images with a focus on head and neck cancer. I am also interested in the domain of trustworthy AI, involving issues such as bias, fairness, interpretability, and ethical considerations in algorithm development and inference. These efforts aim to ensure that AI solutions are not only technically robust but also ethically sound and socially responsible, paving the way for equitable and trustworthy AI applications.
